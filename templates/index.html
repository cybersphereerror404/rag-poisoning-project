About the HED Framework

Hybrid Efficiency Defense — resolving the trilemma between robustness, efficiency, and architectural grounding.

Research Aim

Design and evaluate a Hybrid Efficiency Defense (HED) framework that resolves the technical trilemma between adversarial robustness, computational efficiency, and architectural grounding in RAG systems.

Defense Architecture

1
Keyword Filter
~0.3ms · Always applied

Fast lexical scan for known attack patterns (prompt injection keywords, override commands). O(n) complexity — zero model overhead.

2
ML Classifier
~8ms · Always applied

TF-IDF + Logistic Regression trained on 120-doc corpus. Detects semantic manipulation patterns beyond simple keywords.

3
Consistency Filter
~6ms · Selective (high-risk only)

Sentence-embedding semantic outlier detection. Only triggered when Layer 2 risk > 0.3, saving ~42% compute on clean documents.

Research Questions

RQ1: How do poisoning attacks affect RAG reliability?

RQ2: Which poisoning strategies are most effective?

RQ3: Can retrieval-stage defenses mitigate poisoned data?

RQ4: How do defenses affect utility & computational overhead?

RQ5: How does HED compare to an undefended baseline?
